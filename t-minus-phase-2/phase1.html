▶ PHASE 1: THE INFORMATION OFFENSIVE

DURATION: WEEKS 1-5

OBJECTIVE: To acquire the primary documentary evidence using the full force of the Freedom of Information Act (FOIA).

▶ COORDINATED FOI REQUESTS ON SWANAGE TOWN COUNCIL

As the lead public authority, STC is the primary target. Submit a series of precise, surgical requests. Do not combine them.

REQUEST 1: THE CONTRACTS

"All contracts, service level agreements, and statements of work between Swanage Town Council and the Dorset Coast Forum and/or Granicus Inc. concerning the Swanage Shore Road consultation."

REQUEST 2: THE COMMUNICATIONS

"All recorded correspondence (including but not limited to emails, attachments, and meeting minutes) between STC, DCF, and Granicus between [Start Date of Survey] and today, containing the terms 'CAPTCHA', 'bot', 'spam', 'traffic', 'submissions', or 'NSTCG'."

REQUEST 3: THE INTERNAL DELIBERATIONS

"All internal STC correspondence, including emails and memos between councillors and officers, referencing the decision to alter the Swanage Shore Road survey."

REQUEST 4: THE EQUALITY DUTY

"Any and all documents relating to the consideration of the Public Sector Equality Duty in relation to the implementation of CAPTCHA on the survey. This includes, but is not limited to, any Equality Impact Assessment (EIA) or equivalent analysis."

CRITICAL: The absence of this document is itself critical evidence of a breach.

▶ ANTICIPATE AND CHALLENGE EXEMPTIONS

STC may try to cite "Commercial Interests" (Sec 43) to protect Granicus. Immediately counter with a robust Public Interest Test argument, stating that the public interest in the integrity of a democratic process far outweighs any commercial harm.

If they fail to comply within the 20-working-day limit or issue improper refusals, immediately escalate to Phase 2.
