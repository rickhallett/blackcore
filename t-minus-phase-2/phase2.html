▶ PHASE 2: THE REGULATORY ASSAULT

CONCURRENT WITH PHASE 1

OBJECTIVE: To trigger formal, independent investigations by the statutory bodies responsible for oversight, forcing the targets onto a defensive footing.

▶ FILE FORMAL COMPLAINT WITH THE ICO

COMPLAINT A: FAILURE TO COMPLY WITH FOIA

Lodge this if STC fails in its FOIA duties from Phase 1. The ICO will compel them to respond.

COMPLAINT B: BREACH OF UK GDPR

File a separate, substantive complaint against STC as the "Data Controller" and Granicus as the "Data Processor." Argue that by implementing a discriminatory barrier and altering the process non-transparently, they breached Article 5 of UK GDPR ("processed lawfully, fairly and in a transparent manner").

IMPACT: This puts Granicus in the direct line of fire for ICO enforcement and massive potential fines.

▶ FILE COMPLAINT WITH LOCAL GOVERNMENT OMBUDSMAN

Lodge a complaint against Swanage Town Council for "maladministration causing injustice."

Use the evidence from the documents: the biased process, the lack of transparency, the creation of a contaminated dataset, and the waste of public funds. The injustice is the disenfranchisement of a significant portion of the Swanage community.

▶ SUBMIT EVIDENCE TO THE EHRC

Present the EHRC with a formal briefing outlining a prima facie breach of the Public Sector Equality Duty (PSED) by STC.

Provide the demographic data (Appendix E) and highlight the failure to conduct an impact assessment. The EHRC has the power to launch its own formal investigations into public bodies that fail their equality duties.

CRITICAL: This is a highly aggressive and potent step.
