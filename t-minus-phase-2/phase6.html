▶ PHASE 6: LEGAL ESCALATION

AS HIGH AS NECESSARY FOR ACCOUNTABILITY

OBJECTIVE: To pursue justice through every available legal avenue, escalating through the entire UK court system if necessary to establish precedent and ensure full accountability for the corruption of democratic processes.

▶ JUDICIAL REVIEW PROCEEDINGS

ADMINISTRATIVE COURT

File for Judicial Review at the High Court (Administrative Court) challenging:

  • The lawfulness of STC's decision-making process
  • Breach of the Gunning Principles for consultation
  • Failure to comply with the Public Sector Equality Duty
  • Procedural impropriety in implementing CAPTCHA
  • Wednesbury unreasonableness of the decision

EXPECTED TIMELINE

Permission Stage: 3-6 months
Substantive Hearing: 6-12 months
Judgment: 1-3 months post-hearing

▶ APPEALS PROCESS

COURT OF APPEAL
If High Court decision is unfavorable, appeal on grounds of:
  • Error of law in interpretation
  • Procedural irregularity
  • Wrong application of legal principles
SUPREME COURT
Final domestic appeal if case raises issues of:
  • General public importance
  • Constitutional significance
  • Democratic process integrity

▶ SUPREME COURT STRATEGY

Frame the case as a matter of fundamental democratic importance:

CONSTITUTIONAL PRINCIPLES

  • Right to participate in democratic processes
  • Transparency in public decision-making
  • Protection of vulnerable groups in consultations
  • Accountability of tech companies in democratic infrastructure

PRECEDENT SETTING

Establish binding precedent that:

  • CAPTCHA implementation requires equality impact assessment
  • Mid-consultation changes require transparent justification
  • Tech companies have fiduciary duties in democratic processes
  • Councils cannot delegate democratic functions without oversight

BEYOND UK COURTS

If necessary, prepare for potential escalation to:
European Court of Human Rights
Article 10 (Freedom of Expression)
Article 14 (Prohibition of Discrimination)
Protocol 1, Article 3 (Right to Free Elections)
